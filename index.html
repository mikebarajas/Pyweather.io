PyWeather

The Effects of Latitude on Weather

Summary

Project Summary

PyWeather is a program I wrote that randomly generates coordinates for cities, and then creates an API call to pull the current corresponding weather data from the OpenWeatherMapAPI website. The program exports a CSV of the corressponding data and plots temperature, humidity, windspeed, and cloud cover by latitude.

Comparison

Comparison

Temperature
Latitude is one of the main factors affecting temperature. Latitude is the measurement of the distance of a location on the Earth from the equator. The further away from the equator that you are the less sunlight that this location receives. As expected, higher latitudes have lower temeratures on average.
Humidity
There does not appear to be an observable trend between humidity and latitude. While latitude may impact humidity, it is also influenced strongly by elevation, wind, water currents and proximity to the ocean.
Wind Speed
There does not appear to be an observable trend between wind speed and latitude. Wind speeds are primarily influenced by the pressure-gradient force, the Coriolis force and friction. These factors working together cause the wind to blow in different directions and at different speeds.
Cloud Cover
There does not appear to be an observable trend between cloud cover and latitude. Latitude has little influence in the formation of clouds.
